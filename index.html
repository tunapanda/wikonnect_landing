What is Wikonnect?

Wikonnect seeks to bridge the digital divide through the provision of digital literacy skills.

Users can learn new skills, create content to teach others, and contribute in building the platform as designers or software developers.

Implementing Partners

Learning Lions

Learning Lions (LL) is a gUG non-profit organizations based in Germany and Kenya which creates digital content for Wikonnect. LL also runs training programs derived from TI’s 3-month intensive training program in tech, design, and business in remote Kenya.

Tunapanda Institute

Tunapanda Institute (TI) is a 501(c)3 non-profit organization based in the US and Kenya. TI builds and maintains the core Wikonnect software through our team members and skilled open source volunteers. TI also establishes and funds training programs in low-income areas, such as Kibera.

Funding Partners

GIZ

Wikonnect involves a collaboration between the Strategic Partnership Technology in Africa (SPTA) and the Kenyan digital company BRCK. SPTA is a network connecting German development cooperation and around 200 European companies. Its aim is to develop public-private cooperation projects relating to digitalisation and innovation in Africa

BRCK

BRCK has been developing connectivity technology for emerging markets since 2013. Moja is BRCK’s free public WiFi network. Anyone within range of the signal can connect to the internet for free and access Moja’s stored content to watch shows, listen to music, or learn. Moja is currently available in more than 2,700 WiFi hotspots in Nairobi and Kigali, reaching over 600,000 unique monthly users. BRCK is the main technology, connectivity and distribution partner for this project. BRCK has allocated one million Moja points for the project to distribute and promote Wikonnect as a “learn to earn” model.
